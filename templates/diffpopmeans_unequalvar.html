Comparing two population means (variances unknown and unequal)

We compare two populations means. The data are independent samples from normally distributed populations. We compare and test whether the mean, \(\mu_1\) of population 1 is equal to the mean, \(\mu_2\) of population 2. We consider the difference \(\mu_1 - \mu_2\) whose point estimate is given by the difference of sample means, \(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2\).

In this section, we provide the point estimage, the \((1-\alpha)100\%\) confidence interval of \(\mu_1 - \mu_2\), and perform hypothesis testing if \(\mu_1 = \mu_2\). We consider the population standard deviations \(\sigma_1\) and \(\sigma_2\) to be unknown and unequal. We replace \(\sigma_1\) and \(\sigma_2\) with sample standard deviations, \(s_1\) and \(s_2\). So the random variable \(\displaystyle t = \frac{(\bar{X}_1 - \bar{X}_2) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}} \) follows approximately t-distribution with degree of freedom: \(\displaystyle \Delta = \frac{\left( s_1^2/n_1 + s_2^2/n_2 \right)^2}{ \displaystyle \frac{\left(s_1^2/n_1 \right)^2}{n_1 - 1} + \frac{\left(s_2^2/n_2 \right)^2}{n_2 - 1}} \) rounded down to the nearest integer.

\(\underline{(1-\alpha)100\% \quad \text{Confidence Interval for} \quad \mu_1 - \mu_2}\):

  • \(\displaystyle CI = \Bigg((\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - t_{\alpha/2}.\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}, \quad (\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) + t_{\alpha/2}.\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}\\ \Bigg)\) Where \(t_{\alpha/2}\) is the t-value such that the area to the right is \(\alpha/2\) with DoF, \(\Delta\)
    • OR
  • \(\displaystyle CI = \Big( (\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - ME, \quad (\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) + ME \Big)\),
    where \(ME = t_{\alpha/2}.\sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}\displaystyle \) is the margin of error.

Hypothesis Testing (non pooled t-test:)

We state the hypothesis to prove that if two means are equal or one is greater/smaller than the other.

  1. Two-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu_1 = \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1-\mu_2 = 0\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu_1 \neq \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1 - \mu_2 \neq 0\)
  2. Left-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu_1 = \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1 = \mu_2\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu_1 < \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1-\mu_2 <0 \)
  3. Right-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu_1 = \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1 = \mu_2\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu_1 > \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1-\mu_2>0\)

We have the following values:

  • Sample sizes: \(n_1, n_2\).
  • Sample means: \(\bar{x}_1, \bar{x}_2\)
  • Sample standard deviations: \(s_1, s_2\).
  • Population means: \(\mu_1, \mu_2\).
  • Difference in population means: \(\mu_1 - \mu_2\).
  • Significance level: \(\alpha\).

We find the following values:

  • Test statistic: \(t_{\text{test}} = \displaystyle \frac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{\sqrt{\left( \frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2} \right)}} = \displaystyle \frac{(\bar{x}_ - \bar{x}_2)}{\sqrt{\left( \frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2} \right)}} \) (under the null hypothesis, \(\mu_1 = \mu_2\)).
  • Critical value: \(t_{\text{crit}} \)
  • P-value: \(p\)

Conclusion:

  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if Test Statistic, \(t_{\text{test}}\) is in the non-rejection region. Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
    • OR
  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if P-Value is bigger than \(\alpha\). Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
