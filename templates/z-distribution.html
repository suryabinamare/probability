Z-Distribution

Introduction:

The Z-distribution, also called the standard normal distribution, is a specific form of the normal distribution with a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of 1. Its graph is bell-shaped and symmetric about zero, meaning values equidistant from the center have equal probabilities. The Z-distribution is commonly used in hypothesis testing and confidence interval estimation, particularly when the population standard deviation is known or when the sample size is large enough for the Central Limit Theorem to apply. The total area under the probability density curve equals 1, representing all possible outcomes. In the visualization, the P-value is shown as a blue shaded region under the curve, while the Z-score appears as a red dot along the horizontal axis.

The area under the density curve is 1. P-value represents the area of a region under the curve which is shaded blue. Z-score is plotted along the horizontal axis as the red dot.

  • Z-score: The Z-score represents the standardized value you are analyzing. It tells you how far your observation is from the mean in terms of standard deviation. In this app, the Z-score is plotted on the graph so you can visually see where it lies on the distribution.
  • P-value: The P-value is the probability of observing a value less than or equal to the selected Z-score. It corresponds to the blue shaded area under the curve to the left of the red dot. For \(z_0\), the p-value is given by \(p(z \leq z_0)\).
    If you want to find the area to the right of \(z_0\),
    \(p(z\geq z_0) = 1 - p(z\leq z_0)\)
