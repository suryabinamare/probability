Z-Distribution

Information about Z-distribution:

The Z-distribution, also known as the standard normal distribution, is a special case of the normal distribution with a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of 1. It is symmetric about the vertical line through 0 and has a bell-shaped curve. The Z-distribution is used in hypothesis testing and confidence interval estimation when the population standard deviation is known or when the sample size is large enough to apply the Central Limit Theorem.

The area under the density curve is 1. P-value represents the area of a region under the curve which is shaded blue. Z-score is plotted along the horizontal axis as the red dot.

  • Z-score: This is the value of the Z-statistic you want to evaluate. This value is plotted along the horizontal axis as the red dot. You can find the Z-score when you know the P-value.
  • P-value: This value represents the area under the curve to the left of the Z-score and is shaded blue in the plot. For \(z_0\), the p-value is given by \(p(z \leq z_0)\).
    If you want to find the area to the right of \(z_0\),
    \(p(z\geq z_0) = 1 - p(z\leq z_0)\)
