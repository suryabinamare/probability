Goodness of Fit Test (Chi-Square)

A goodness of fit test checks whether observed data follow a specific theoretical distribution. It compares observed frequencies with expected frequencies to determine whether any differences are likely due to random variation. One of the most common methods is the Chi-square goodness-of-fit test.

An example: Is the Die Fair?

We roll a six-sided die 150 times to test whether all sides are equaly likely (each face has 1/6 chances of landing).

Hypotheses:

  • \(H_0\): the die is fair (\(p_1 = p_2 = p_3 = p_4 = p_5 = p_6 = 1/6\))
  • \(H_1\): The die is not fair (at least one probability differs)

Observed vs Expected Frequencies:

  • Observed: [23, 26, 23, 21, 31, 26]
  • Expected (fair die): [25, 25, 25, 25, 25, 25] = 150 rolls
    since \(150 \times \frac{1}{6} = 25.\)

Chi-Square Statistic:

The test statistic is computed using:

\(\displaystyle \chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O-E)^2}{E}\)

\(\displaystyle \chi^2 = \frac{(23-25)^2}{25} + \frac{(26-25)^2}{25} + \frac{(23-25)^2}{25}+ \frac{(21-25)^2}{25} + \frac{(31-25)^2}{25} + \frac{(26-25)^2}{25}\)

\(\displaystyle \chi^2 = 2.48\)

Decision:

At a 5% significance level:

  • Using Chi-Square distribution with degreee of freedom = k - 1 = 5, Critical value = 11.07
  • P-value = 0.78
  • If \(\chi^2 > \text{critical value} \rightarrow \text{reject H_0}\)
  • If \(\chi^2 \leq \text{critical value} \rightarrow \text{fail to reject H_0}\)

OR

  • If P-value \(\leq \alpha \rightarrow \text{reject H_0} \)
  • If P-value \(> \alpha \rightarrow \text{fail to reject H_0} \)

Conclusion:

The die is fair.

Hypothesis Testing: Goodness of Fit Test:

  • Null Hypothesis (H0): The observed data follows the expected distribution.
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H1): The observed data does not follow the expected distribution.

Steps:

  1. State the null and alternative hypotheses.
  2. Choose a significance level (α).
  3. Calculate the expected frequencies = n * p, where n is the total number of observations and p is the probability for each category.
  4. Compute the chi-square statistic = Σ((O - E)² / E), where O is the observed frequency and E is the expected frequency.
  5. Determine the critical value or p-value.
  6. Make a decision to reject or fail to reject the null hypothesis.
