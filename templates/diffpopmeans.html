Comparing two population means (variances unknown but equal)

We compare means of two populations. The sample of population 1 is independent of the sample of population 2. We apply these samples and statistical tools to test whether the mean of a population 1 is equal to the mean of population 2. We also estimate the confidence inteval for the difference to means. We assume that the standard deviations are equal but unknow.

We consider the sample mean \(\bar{x_1}\) of population 1 whose population mean is \(\mu_1\) and population standard deviation \(\sigma_1\) and sample mean \(\bar{x_2}\) of population 2 whose population mean is \(\mu_2\) and population standard deviation \(\sigma_2\)

We consider the difference \(\bar{X_1} - \bar{X_2}\) that estimates the difference in population means \(\mu_1 - \mu_2\). The random variable \(\bar{X_1} - \bar{X_2}\) follows a normal distribution with mean \(\mu_1 - \mu_2\) and the standard deviation is \(\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{\sigma_2^2}{n_2}}\).
So, \(\displaystyle z = \frac{(\bar{X_1} - \bar{X_2}) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{\sigma_2^2}{n_2}}}\) follows a standard normal distribution, where \(\bar{X_1}\) and \(\bar{X_2}\) are the sample means, \(\sigma_1\) and \(\sigma_2\) are the population standard deviations, and \(n_1\) and \(n_2\) are the sample sizes.

We consider the following cases to estimate the standard deviations.

  • When two population standard deviations are equal but unknown, (\(\sigma_1 = \sigma_2 = \sigma\)). \begin{align*} z &= \frac{(\bar{X_1} - \bar{X_2}) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{\sqrt{\left( \frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{\sigma_2^2}{n_2} \right)}}\\ &= \frac{(\bar{X_1} - \bar{X_2}) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{\sqrt{\sigma^2\left( \frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2} \right)}}\\ &= \frac{(\bar{X_1} - \bar{X_2}) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{\sqrt{s_p^2\left( \frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2} \right)}}\\ &= \frac{(\bar{X_1} - \bar{X_2}) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{s_p\sqrt{\left( \frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2} \right)}} \end{align*} Where we estimate the common population standard deviation \(\sigma\) using the sample standard deviations \(s_p = \sqrt{\frac{(n_1 - 1)s_1^2 + (n_2 - 1)s_2^2}{n_1 + n_2 - 2}}\): Thus, the random variable, \[t = \frac{(\bar{X_1} - \bar{X_2}) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{s_p\sqrt{\left( \frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2} \right)}}\] follows a t-distribution with \(n_1 + n_2 - 2\) degrees of freedom.

\(\underline{ (1-\alpha)100\% \quad \text{Confidence Interval for} \quad \mu_1-\mu_2}\):

  • CI = \(\displaystyle \Bigg( (\bar{x_1} - \bar{x_2}) - t_{\alpha/2}.s_p.\sqrt{\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2}}, \quad (\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) + t_{\alpha/2}.s_p.\sqrt{\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2}} \Bigg)\)

    • or
  • CI = \(\Big( (\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - ME,\quad (\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) + ME \Big)\),

    • Where,
    • \(ME = t_{\alpha/2}.s_p.\sqrt{\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2}}\) = margin of error:
    • \(t_{\alpha/2}\) is the $t$-value for which the area to the right of it under densisty curve is \(\alpha/2\)
    • \(s_p = \sqrt{\frac{(n_1 - 1)s_1^2 + (n_2 - 1)s_2^2}{n_1 + n_2 - 2}} \) = pooled sample standard deviation.

Hypothesis Testing (pooled t-test:)

We state the hypothesis to prove that if two means are equal or one is greater than the other.

  1. Two-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu_1 = \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1-\mu_2 = 0\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu_1 \neq \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1 - \mu_2 \neq 0\)
  2. Left-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu_1 = \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1 = \mu_2\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu_1 < \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1-\mu_2 <0 \)
  3. Right-tailed Test:
    • \(H_0 : \quad \mu_1 = \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1 = \mu_2\)
    • \(H_a : \quad \mu_1 > \mu_2\)     or     \(\mu_1-\mu_2>0\)


We have the following values:

  • Sample sizes: \(n_1, n_2\).
  • Degree of Freedom (DF) = \(n_1 + n_2 - 2\)
  • Sample means: \(\bar{x}_1, \bar{x}_2\)
  • Sample standard deviations: \(s_1, s_2\).
  • Population means: \(\mu_1, \mu_2\).
  • Significance level: \(\alpha\).

We find the following values:

  • Test statistic: \( t_{\text{test}} =\displaystyle \frac{(\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{s_p\sqrt{\left( \frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2} \right)}} = \displaystyle \frac{(\bar{x}_ - \bar{x}_2)}{s_p\sqrt{\left( \frac{1}{n_1} + \frac{1}{n_2} \right)}} \) (under the null hypothesis, (since \(\mu_1 = \mu_2 \)))
  • Critical value: \(t_{\text{crit}}\)
  • P-value: \(p\)

Conclusion:

  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if Test Statistic, \(t_{\text{test}}\) is in the non-rejection region. Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
    • OR
  • Do not reject \(H_0\) if P-Value is bigger than \(\alpha\). Else reject \(H_0\) in favor of alternative hypothesis.
